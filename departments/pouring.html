Pouring Department

The Pouring Department is where molten metal is carefully poured into the prepared sand moulds. This process requires precision to ensure that the metal fills the mould cavity completely, avoiding defects such as air pockets, cold shuts, or misruns. Proper pouring techniques contribute directly to the strength and quality of the final casting.

Pouring Techniques

Depending on the casting size and type, different pouring methods are used including gravity pouring, pressure pouring, and vacuum pouring. Operators must control pouring speed, temperature, and flow rate to minimize turbulence and oxidation. Use of pouring ladles, funnels, and gating systems ensures smooth and controlled flow of molten metal.

Safety Measures

Due to the extreme temperatures involved, the Pouring Department enforces strict safety protocols. Workers wear heat-resistant clothing, face shields, and gloves. The workplace is equipped with emergency cooling systems and fire suppression tools to handle any unforeseen spills or accidents. Regular training is conducted to maintain a safe working environment.

Quality Control

Pouring accuracy is critical for defect-free castings. The department works closely with quality inspectors to monitor pouring temperatures and timings. Samples are taken periodically to check for impurities and microstructure quality. Advanced monitoring systems are also installed to automate and optimize the pouring process.

Collaboration & Continuous Improvement

The Pouring Department collaborates with pattern, moulding, and finishing teams to ensure a seamless casting process. Continuous improvement efforts focus on adopting new pouring technologies, reducing metal waste, and enhancing environmental safety through emission controls and recycling molten metal when possible.
