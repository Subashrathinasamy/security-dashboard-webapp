Decoring Department

The Decoring Department is responsible for removing cores from castings after solidification to create hollow sections or internal cavities required by the design.

Core Removal Process

After casting, cores are carefully extracted either mechanically or chemically, depending on the core material. Proper removal is crucial to avoid damage to the casting.

Equipment and Techniques

The department uses vibratory machines, shot blasting, or chemical solvents to dissolve or loosen core materials. Skilled technicians ensure precision and care during decoring.

Quality Control

Post decoring, the castings are inspected for core residue, surface defects, and dimensional accuracy. Thorough cleaning prepares castings for further finishing.

Importance in Production

Decoring is essential to achieve the intended casting shape and functional properties. The department collaborates closely with pattern and knockout teams to ensure smooth operations.
